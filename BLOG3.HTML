What is hacktoberfest and why you should care ?

Hacktoberfest is an annual event that takes place every October and encourages people to contribute to open-source projects on platforms like GitHub. It was started by DigitalOcean in 2014 and has since been joined by partners like GitHub, Twilio, and DEV.

To participate in Hacktoberfest, you need to make four pull requests (PRs) to any public GitHub repository between October 1 and October 31. PRs can be made to any repository, not just ones that are participating in Hacktoberfest.

If you complete the challenge, you can earn a limited-edition T-shirt and some digital swag. But more importantly, Hacktoberfest is an opportunity to learn new skills, improve your coding abilities, and contribute to open-source software that benefits the wider community.

By participating in Hacktoberfest, you can help improve the quality of open-source software, make connections with other developers, and gain valuable experience in collaborating on software projects. It's a great way to give back to the community and learn new things at the same time.
